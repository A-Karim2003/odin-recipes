Lasagne

What is Lasagne

Lasagna is the name of one of the oldest and best-known pasta shapes. It is usually rectangular or ribbon shaped,thicker than tagliatelle, made from a dough based on flour and eggs, with numerous local variants. After being boiled, the rectangular lasagna noodles are drained and placed in layers with a filling that varies based on different local traditions.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 celery sticks, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot (about 100g), finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 140g cubetti di pancetta
  • 500g beef mince (we used 10% fat)
  • 200ml milk
  • 2 x 400g cans chopped tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 rosemary sprig
  • 2 thyme sprigs
  • 2 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 beef stock cubes

For the lasagne

  • about 400g dried pasta sheets
  • 50g parmesan, finely grated

For the white sauce (béchamel)

  • 1.5l milk
  • 1 onion, thickly sliced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 3 cloves
  • 100g butter
  • 100g plain flour
  • good grating of nutmeg

Steps for making lasagne

  1. Make the béchamel sauce first. In a large saucepan, combine the milk, onion, bay leaves, and cloves and bring to a gentle boil. Turn off the heat and leave to infuse for 1 hour.
  2. In a separate big pot, combine the oil, celery, onion, carrot, garlic, and pancetta for the meat sauce. Cook until the vegetables are tender but not browned.
  3. Combine the beef and milk and chopped tomatoes in a mixing bowl. Using a wooden spoon, combine the ingredients and break up and crush the mince lumps against the pan's sides. Stir in all of the herbs, stock cubes until the mince is mostly broken down, and bring to a boil. Cook for 1 hour, stirring occasionally to prevent the bottom from catching.
  4. Uncover and continue to gently boil for 30 minutes to an hour, or until the meat is cooked and the sauce is thick. Seasoning and flavour
  5. Pour the milk through a fine sieve into one or two jugs to complete the béchamel sauce. Melt the butter in the same pan, then stir in the flour with a wooden spoon and cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Stir in the filtered milk a bit at a time and the mixture will thicken to a doughy paste at first, but keep going, adding milk gradually to prevent lumps. Bring to a slow simmer, stirring regularly (if you have lumps, give it a quick whisk). Gently bubble for a few minutes, or until thickened. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg to taste.
  7. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celsius/160 degrees Celsius fan/gas 4. Spread a tablespoon of the beef sauce onto the bottom of a 3.5-litre baking dish. Cover with a single layer of dry pasta sheets,snapping them together to fit if necessary, and top with a quarter of the béchamel. Spread a third of the meat sauce on top, followed by a sprinkle of parmesan.
  8. To utilise all of the meat sauce, repeat the layers of pasta, béchamel, meat, and parmesan two more times. Finish with a final layer of spaghetti, the leftover béchamel, and the parmesan. Place the dish on a baking pan to collect spillage and bake for 1 hour, or until the top is bubbling, golden, and crisp.
